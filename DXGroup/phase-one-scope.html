Agreed Phase One Scope

Product Experience Assessment Framework

The Digital Experience Architecture (DXA) team will spend the next 12 months building a rigorous internal audit capability to prove measurable value before integrating the framework into Aramco’s broader governance model.

Focus: Establish clear evaluation criteria, run quarterly audits, report insights, and offer an advisory audit service. Process enforcement, automation, and tooling will be deferred to later phases.

Engagement Principles

  • Phase One centers on DXA-led audits and learning, not enterprise enforcement.
  • Deliverables emphasize clarity, transparency, and defensible decision-making.
  • Outputs must be easy to scale into governance once value is demonstrated.

Core Deliverables

1

Evaluation Parameters & Criteria

  • Define measurable parameters for auditing digital products.
  • Anchor criteria in WCAG 2.1, Nielsen usability heuristics, and ISO 9241.
  • Create a weighted scoring methodology that produces clear, defensible ratings.
  • Document evaluation steps to ensure consistency across auditors.
2

Product Selection Methodology

  • Develop a transparent formula to decide which products enter the audit queue.
  • Prioritize using user base size, business criticality, risk exposure, support volume, and other data points.
  • Provide rationale for selections so stakeholders understand why their product was or was not audited.
3

Internal Audit Process

  • DXA team conducts quarterly audits on the prioritized products.
  • Produce evaluation checklists, testing procedures, and evidence collection standards.
  • Target 3–5 product audits per quarter to build meaningful baseline coverage.
4

Quarterly Management Reporting

  • Deliver executive-ready reports covering findings, inconsistencies, and recommendations.
  • Share product-by-product results plus trend analysis as the dataset matures.
  • Highlight actionable insights leadership can use to prioritize investment.
5

Audit Request Service

  • Create a simple mechanism for product teams to request voluntary audits.
  • DXA reviews, schedules, and delivers audit reports as an advisory service.
  • Intentionally non-enforcement: the goal is enablement and partnership.

Cadence & Operating Model

  • Quarterly rhythm: Select products, perform assessments, and publish management report.
  • Evidence standard: Screenshots, annotated findings, metrics, and remediation guidance captured for each audit.
  • Stakeholder touchpoints: Kickoff per audit, weekly check-ins during testing, and readouts with product owners.
  • Success definition: Establish trusted baseline data that demonstrates the value of a broader framework rollout.

Deferred for Future Phases

  • Embedding the framework into organization-wide delivery processes and mandatory checkpoints.
  • Building ServiceNow workflows or other automation layers.
  • Formal governance models or compliance enforcement mechanisms.
  • Procurement or integration of specialized tooling.
  • Enterprise-wide compliance requirements or policy changes.

These investments will be revisited after Phase One demonstrates measurable value and gains executive sponsorship for expansion.
