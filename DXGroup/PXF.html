Executive Summary

The Problem

  • Digital products launch without standardized UX validation.
  • Inconsistent user experiences harm productivity.
  • No baseline metrics exist to validate improvement claims.
  • Compliance gaps persist across accessibility and usability standards.
  • Post-launch rework drives avoidable cost and delay.

The Solution

Deploy a two-phase Product Experience Assessment Framework that introduces systematic, standards-based validation for every digital product.

  • Phase One (12 months): Lay the foundation using WCAG 2.1 AA, Nielsen heuristics, and ISO 9241.
  • Phase Two (12-18 months): Layer oil & gas-specific requirements once baseline is established.

Expected Outcomes

  • 100% product compliance with UX standards prior to launch.
  • Baseline quality metrics captured across the digital portfolio.
  • Self-service toolkit enables independent assessment by teams.
  • ServiceNow-integrated audit trail ensures governance.

Phase One: Foundation (12 Months)

Core Framework Components

Three Assessment Dimensions

  1. Accessibility Compliance: WCAG 2.1 Level AA baseline.
  2. Usability Quality: Evaluation through Nielsen heuristics and ISO 9241.
  3. Performance Standards: Industry benchmarks aligned to enterprise expectations.

Two Strategic Checkpoints

  1. Design Review: Conducted before development begins.
  2. Pre-Launch Audit: Completed two weeks prior to deployment.

Self-Service Toolkit

  • Assessment templates (Word/Excel).
  • Step-by-step checklists and evidence guides.
  • ServiceNow submission forms for approvals.

Governance Model

  • Teams self-assess using the toolkit.
  • Evidence submitted via ServiceNow for CoE review.
  • Launch blocked until approval is granted.

Why Build on Existing Standards?

Immediate Benefits

  • Established credibility with 30+ years of validation.
  • Faster delivery by adapting proven guidance.
  • Defensible, objective criteria reduce debate.
  • Lower resistance thanks to familiar concepts.

Phase Two Evolution

  • Adopt ISA-101 for control systems.
  • Account for limited connectivity in field operations.
  • Integrate safety-critical system standards.
  • Reflect energy-sector operational realities.

Implementation Timeline

Quarter 1: Discovery & Foundation

  • Conduct 15-20 stakeholder interviews.
  • Validate standards-based approach.
  • Design framework specifications.

Deliverable: Framework Design + Interview Findings.

Quarter 2: Build & Validate

  • Create toolkit templates and guidance.
  • Configure ServiceNow integration.
  • Validate with early adopters.

Deliverable: Complete Toolkit + Operational ServiceNow.

Quarter 3: Pilot & Refine

  • Run 3-5 pilot products through checkpoints.
  • Collect usage data and feedback.
  • Refine framework based on learning.

Deliverable: Pilot Results + Framework Refinements.

Quarter 4: Scale & Establish

  • Train organization-wide.
  • Roll out mandatory compliance.
  • Monitor adoption and support teams.

Deliverable: Operational Framework + Phase Two Recommendations.

Key Decision Gates

  • Month 6: Approve toolkit + ServiceNow readiness.
  • Month 9: Approve organizational rollout.
  • Month 12: Approve Phase Two scope and investment.

Recommendation

  • Standards-based approach delivers immediate credibility.
  • Modest investment for high ROI potential.
  • Phased risk management proves value before expansion.
  • Timeline accounts for capacity constraints.
  • Builds lasting organizational capability.
  • Addresses a critical governance gap.
